Education & Working Experiences

In 2016, I entered Shanghaitech University, majoring in computer science. After the first year's basic computer science courses, I got interested in coding, especially on projects with beautiful visual effect. In spare time, I also learned the skills of using game engines by myself. After that, I made sure my aspiration in Computer Graphics and Game Development. I actively participated in the game technical lectures and gamejams. Also, I took several electives in Computer Graphics and Computer Vision. These courses and activities helped me to find my interest in rendering technique.

Art is another interest of mine. Fortunately, ShanghaiTech founded the School of Creativity and Art(SCA) in 2017. I was among the first group of students who took courses of SCA. During the whole college time, I had taken Western Art History, Entertainment Design, User Experience Design and many other courses. And I became the teaching assistant of Experimental Animation course in Spring 2019.

Beyond the courses, I tried to find opportunities to work in game industry.In the summer of 2018, I got my first practice opportunity in Shanghai Hyron Software. I worked as level designer intern in a game studio of Hyron for two months, produced a project named 'Little Fist Combat'.Unfortunately, this game did not make it to the market due to the game license issues. Nevertheless, I learned the operations of a small game studio in China through this internship experience.

Because of winning the First Prize of NetEase College Mini Game Competetion, I got a technical artist intern offer in 2019 summer. NetEase is the second biggest game company in China. At NetEase, I firstly came into contact with the job of technical art. On this internship, I was asked to use commercial game engines to achieve the effect of visual designer in the pre-research for the project's demo, I found that such big game projects require a lot of job posts with a variety of expertise for subtasks. Therefore, I am motivated to learn a wide range of topics in game development.

After I graduated from ShanghaiTech in July 2020, I become a technique artist of User Research Department in NetEase Shanghai now. In my current job as a TA, I begin to use the home-grown game engine of NetEase. After participating in several tasks of online production, although some productions are famous in this industry, I find many limitations and inefficiencies works in current game production pipeline. So, I aspire to make games in a more flexible way. Thus, I will pursue a graduate study to gain advanced knowledge and develop new techniques.
